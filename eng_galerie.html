The great french feminists

Olympe de Gouges

French woman of letters. She became politician and wrote the Declaration of the Rights of Women and Citizens in 1791. She also fought for the right to vote for women.

Simone de Beauvoir

A major feminist theorist and philosopher. She became known for her book "The Second Sex" published in 1949. Simone de Beauvoir participated in the women's liberation movement in the 1970s.

Simone Veil

She was health Minister under President Valéry Giscard d'Estaing. She allowed the publication of the law of January 17 in 1975 (Veil law), relating to the voluntary interruption of pregnancy, so which allowing a decriminalization of abortion in France.

International figures by category

Ecology

Vandana Shiva

India - born in 1952.

Great figure of ecofeminism.

Education

Angela Davis

USA - born in 1944.

Feminist and communist activist, she defends human rights.

She is also a writer and philosophy professor.

Sport

Clarisse Agbégnénou

France - born in 1992.

French judoka who participated in the 2020 Summer Olympics.

She won two gold medals.

Art

Marina Abramović

Serbia - born in 1946.

She is an artist who is part of the body art movement. She can be defined as a feminist.

Science

June Almeida

United Kingdom - born in 1930 and died in 2007.

She discovered and developed the first description of a coronavirus through an ingenious electron microscopy technique.

Medecine

Helen Taussig

USA - born in 1898 and died in 1986.

Great American pediatrician, she succeeded in curing the tetralogy of Fallot (blue child disease) in infants.

Politics

Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez

USA - born in 1989.

United States Representative since 2019. Great national and international figure, very active on Twitter in particular.

Legal

Gisèle Halimi

Tunisia / France - born in 1927 and died in 2020.

Tunisian of origin, Gisèle Halimi worked in France as a lawyer. She was also a feminist activist.
