Ecofeminism : the ecological feminist movement.

Reading time : about 3 minutes.

Have you ever heard of ecofeminism? This philosophical current is based on two movements : ecologism and feminism. Ecologism is the political struggle to protect Nature (including humans) from pollution and the causes of climate imbalance. Feminism encompasses the movements defending mainly the rights and respect of women. Knowing this, how did these two political themes became one, and who founded it? These topics are not opposed, not at all. We will discover why.

Ecofeminism : a kind of feminism that defends Nature

This current of thought, born in the seventies, is based on a very precise fact. It’s to affirm that Nature is alive (for example, trees are protectors, and not a consumable material). It is thanks to it that we live, that the human species endures. It follows the "agricultural law" : the seed gives right to the harvest ; Nature feeds Man.

Moreover, Nature is made of complex interconnections : forests are linked to water currents, species feed each other species... This allows the living beings to live, to survive.

The woman is also alive and has the same effect of the Nature : to prolong the existence of the humans. The female gender is therefore linked to the Earth. This is why, statistically, women are more ecological than men.
There is however another reason, which we will detail later.

Women and Nature are furthermore linked by another thing, more important phenomenon. They are victims of patriarchy and capitalism, of industrialization (destructive elements for Nature and oppressive for women).

Through ecological sciences and different movements (like the Chipko movement), ecofeminists will try to protect women and Nature, in order to regain their freedom. There are many founders of this movement, but here are some important names to know : Vandana Shiva, Françoise d'Eaubonne, and Greta Thunberg.

The power of the feminine principle

The feminine principle (according to ecofeminism) is based on the power to block the negative. It also has a positive power : creativity. The creations and intelligence of women have been recognized since the dawn of time. The feminine principle would even be the creator of the universe, because it is the female who allows the birth of the species.

Even if ecofeminism puts the woman in the foreground, we must remember one thing : the fights for ecology and feminism concerns everyone, and not only women.

Ecofeminism, a committed current

The marginalization of women in society and the destruction of diversity are both linked. It’s these two problems that ecofeminism denounces, and it’s used to lead a fight in order to eradicate these dangers, or at least mitigate them.

For example, the adherents of the movement will denounce the use of "GMOs" (chemical fertilizers), the deforestation, the development of industry, colonizations and the domination of multinationals... And will also seek to reach fair and regular salaries between women and men, put an end to patriarchy...

These fights are based on Gandhi's principles : non-violence (the absence of violence) and non-cooperation (refusing to obey to laws).

Finally, women are more committed to ecology than men because of education, especially.
Women in today's society are educated to be caring, empathetic and ecologically sensitive.

Men are encouraged to be competitive, to take risks.

However, society (with men and women) is taking another turn : it tends to become more and more ecologically committed. It remains to be seen if its evolution remains constant or if things change again, year after year...
