"Contre-culture confiture" : the chronicles of Agathe Parmentier

Reading time : about 3 minutes.

Agathe Parmentier, aka "Ismène de Beauvoir," was born in 1984 in France. Before leaving the country, she published in 2013 a collection of chronicles that she named "Contre-culture confiture".

Discover in this article what these chronicles are about.

The life of Ismène de Beauvoir in "Contre-culture confiture"

The author shares in her book the different experiences she lived during her childhood, her adolescence, and her "adult" life.
Through different memories, she describes the behavior of the society she knew with a lot of subjectivity. Whether it is about her family, past love stories, society in general... By numerous cultural references (belonging to her memories or coming from research), she illustrates her analyses made of judgments. It is mostly references about music (rock, indie, French songs, "underground hype"…), but we can find quotes (from philosophers, authors), and others.

Ismène de Beauvoir tries to position herself in this generation Y. We learn that, for her, the best way to talk about others is to talk about herself.

This collection of chronicles is like a diary where the author releases her thoughts. Indeed, under her pseudonym of feminist writer, she allows herself to express her thinks and to externalize her criticisms without constraints. This is different from her conduct in society, she explains.

In the first part of her book, she also talks about work, her ambitions. There is even a kind of "hymn to boredom", where the author praises it (having been confronted with boredom a lot as a child).

Agathe Parmentier, through a kind of autobiographical collection, approaches serious issues with a great lightness, while having a great editorial quality. We have seen that it deals with society, work, music ; now let's look at the case of feminism, a theme also addressed in this collection.

The development of his feminism

We see it by her pseudonym of author : we can consider Agathe Parmentier as feminist. Or rather, she is a feminist without claiming it. The association of the names Ismène (Antigone's sister) and Simone de Beauvoir is not a coincidence, indeed. The writer recognizes herself completely in these characters, especially Ismène, whom she describes as "the reasonable l'anti-héroïne".

By quoting from her diaries, or by recounting different romantic relationships, she describes how she defined herself in relation to others during her adolescence. Her feminist ideas were forged by encounters made during a lot of trips, especially to Australia.

Therefore, she addresses the themes of pornography (which she sees as a lack of respect towards women), sex, sex education : what she calls "pop-porn".

However, she does not defend the equality between men and women : it’s too ideal and demanding for being reached, according to her. She does not define herself as "pro-sex" either. In fact, she does not belong to a particular branch of feminism. Rather, she knows "what a feminist she is not," she shares.

What about the writer today?

After "Contre-culture confiture", this former press agent moved to Japan, to Tokyo precisely. In her new book, "Pourquoi Tokyo ?", Ismène de Beauvoir tells of her new lifestyle, her anecdotes.

She depicts a culture from a particular angle : her description breaks the stereotypes that we, Occident, have. She is also active on social networks like Twitter, Instagram or Facebook.
